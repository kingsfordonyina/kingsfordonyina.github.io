About me

Welcome! I am a Ph.D. candidate in Biostatistics at the University of Nebraska Medical Center, advised by Dr. Yeongjin Gwon. I expect to defend in 2026 and am on the academic and industry job market.

My research develops scalable Bayesian methods for spatio-temporal data, motivated by problems in environmental health. My dissertation introduces the SVD-PICAR framework — a family of compressed basis-function methods that make hierarchical Bayesian spatio-temporal models computationally practical at large scale. I extend the framework to Gaussian, multinomial, and count outcomes, and to Bayesian Kernel Machine Regression for estimating the health effects of complex environmental mixtures.

As a Graduate Research Assistant in the UNMC Water, Climate and Health Program, I work at the intersection of statistics, climate, and public health, building models that connect environmental exposures to health outcomes. Before my doctoral studies I earned an M.Sc. in Mathematics from the University of Cincinnati, an M.Sc. in Statistics and Mathematics from East Tennessee State University, and a B.Sc. in Mathematical Science (Statistics and Computer Science) from the University of Ghana.
